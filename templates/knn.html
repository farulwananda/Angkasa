K - Nearest Neighbors

The K - Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithm assumes that similar things exist in close proximity. In other words, similar things are near to each other. Similar data points are close to each other. The KNN algorithm hinges on this assumption being true enough for the algorithm to be useful. KNN captures the idea of similarity (sometimes called distance, proximity, or closeness).
